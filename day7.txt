 APTITUDE (Probability)

Question 1
What is the probability of getting a head when tossing a coin?
Solution:
Total outcomes {Head, Tail} = 2
Favorable = 1 
Probability =1/2

Question 2
What is the probability of getting a number greater than 4 when rolling a dice?
Solution:
possible numbers = {1,2,3,4,5,6)
Probability = 2/6=1/3

Question 3
A bag contains 3 red balls and 2 blue balls.
Find probability of picking a red ball.
Solution:
Red=3,blue= 2
total=3+2=5
probability=3/5

Question 4
If two coins are tossed, what is probability of getting two heads?
Solution:
Total outcomes = 4 ({HH, HT, TH, TT})
Favorable (Two Heads) = 1 ({HH})
Probability = 1/4

Question 5
Probability of selecting a vowel from the word ENGINEER.
Solution:
word = ENGINEER
total letters = 8
vowel = 4
probability = 4/8=1/2

COMMUNICATION PRACTICE

Speak for 3 minutes on:

Explain how the Internet works

Include:
- What happens when you type a URL
- DNS
- Server
- Response

Everything starts when you type a web address (like www.google.com) into your 
browser and hit Enter. This address is called a URL.
However, computers don’t understand names; they only understand numbers, which we call IP Addresses.
DNS (Domain Name System)
Because you don’t want to memorize long strings of numbers (like 142.250.190.46), your computer uses the DNS.
Your browser sends a quick message to a DNS Server, which acts like a giant digital phonebook.
The DNS looks up "google.com" and sends back the exact IP address of the computer where that website lives.
Now that your browser has the "address," it sends a request across a massive network of cables to a Server.
Once the server receives your request, it approves it and sends the website back in the form of Data Packets.
Finally, your browser receives all those little "bricks" and puts them back together perfectly on your screen.
