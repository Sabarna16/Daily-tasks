 APTITUDE (Time & Work)

Question 1
A can complete a work in 10 days and B in 15 days.
How many days will they take together?
Solution:
A work = 10 days, B work = 15 days
A+B=1/10+1/15=(3+2)/30=5/30=6days

Question 2
A alone can do a work in 12 days.
After working for 4 days, B joins and work finishes in 4 more days.
Find B's efficiency.
Solution:
A work in 12 days
Work done by A 8*1/12=2/3
work done by B 1-2/3=1/3

Question 3
If 5 people can complete a work in 20 days,
how many people are needed to complete the work in 10 days?
Solution:
5 people complete a work in 20 days
5*20 = 100
100/10=10people

Question 4
A is twice as efficient as B.
If B takes 12 days to complete work, how many days will A take?
Solution:
B take  12 days
A take  as 12/2= 6days

Question 5
Pipe A fills a tank in 6 hours and Pipe B fills in 8 hours.
How long will both take together?
Solution:
pipe A = 1/6
pipe B = 1/8
both A+B = 1/6+1/8= 7/24

COMMUNICATION PRACTICE

Speak for 3 minutes on:

Explain what happens when you run a program

Include:
- Compilation / Interpretation
- Memory allocation
- Execution
- Output

When you run a program,
a compiler or interpreter first translates your code
into machine-readable instructions. The Operating System then 
loads these instructions into RAM (Memory Allocation) and assigns a 
CPU to execute them step-by-step. Finally, the CPU processes the data and 
sends the results to your screen as Output.
